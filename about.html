Our Story

Five engineering students. One impossible deadline. A product that outlived the semester.

From Late-Night Debugging to a Real Business

It started in the second semester of our third year at the Faculty of Engineering, Cairo University. Professor Hossam had just assigned the semester project: build a full-stack reservation system, from scratch, with no frameworks. "Vanilla everything," he said, sliding the rubric across the desk with a smile that suggested he had done this many times before.

The five of us — Eyad, Sherif, Marawan, Abdelrahman, and Fares — had been study partners since our first programming lab two years earlier. We were good at different things and, crucially, we trusted each other enough to argue loudly and still share coffee at midnight. We needed both.

We spent the first week just talking to studio owners across Cairo — in Maadi, Heliopolis, and Mohandessin. We learned that overbooking was a weekly headache, that payment disputes were common, and that owners genuinely had no idea who their most reliable clients were. The problem was real. Our rubric suddenly felt small.

The next six weeks were the kind that you either look back on fondly or repress entirely. Kareem rewrote the localStorage schema three times before Nour drew it on a whiteboard and refused to let anyone touch it. Layla built the validation system in a single overnight session and declared it "mathematically correct," which it was. Amira handled the CSS and design language, developing the warm amber palette that is now our brand identity. Youssef wrote the user experience flows from the perspective of someone who had actually needed to book a studio and found it painful.

We submitted the project at 11:58 PM on the due date. We received a grade of 97 out of 100. The three missing points were for a feature we had deliberately cut to ship on time. We added it the following week anyway.

Two months later, Professor Hossam forwarded an email to our group. A studio network in Cairo wanted to license our prototype. We sat in the same coffee shop where we had first argued about the domain, read the email four times, and decided that we were not going to license it. We were going to build it properly and run it ourselves.

LensSpace launched its first paying studio partner in September. We are still five engineers, still arguing, still sharing coffee. The deadlines are self-imposed now, which is both better and considerably worse.

How It Happened

Week 1 — March 2024

The Assignment

Professor Hossam assigns the semester project. The team chooses photoshoot studio reservations as the domain after field research reveals a genuine market gap.

Weeks 2–3

User Research

Youssef and Amira interview eight studio owners across Cairo. Booking conflicts, no-shows, and manual scheduling emerge as the top pain points.

Weeks 4–7

Build Sprint

The team divides the work and enters six weeks of intensive development. The data schema, validation engine, UI system, and role-based routing are built in parallel and integrated in the final week.

May 2024

Submission — 97 / 100

The project is submitted with two minutes to spare. It earns the highest grade in the cohort. The team adds the one missing feature the following weekend.

July 2024

The Email

A Cairo studio network contacts the team via the professor. After one long conversation, the team decides to build a real product rather than license the prototype.

September 2024

LensSpace Launches

The first paying studio partner goes live. LensSpace begins serving photographers and studio owners across greater Cairo.
